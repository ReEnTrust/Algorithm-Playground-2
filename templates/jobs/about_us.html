Overview of E-Recruitment Systems

E-recruitment facilitates employers in terms of selecting the right candidates from a vast pool of candidates. Likewise, it facilitates job seekers in terms of finding revelant jobs efficiently.

Existing e-recruitment systems use keywords or faceted searches to provide better search results to different categories of users (including employers, job seekers, organisations etc.). A recruitment process involves posting of a job advert with a job description by an employer and a job search and job application by a job seeker. Different types algorithms are embedded in an e-recruitment system to automate this process.

Mostly, e-recruitment systems use recommender, prediction and filtering algorithms among others. These algorithms take specific inputs from different categories of user to produce results. For instance, an employer's primary interest is on the ranking of potential candidates for the advertised job position and hence, an employers give specific inputs (such as the required experience, education level etc) to the algorithms to get desribale results. Likewise, a job seeker provides inputs that fits its profile to the algorithms to get desirable results.
