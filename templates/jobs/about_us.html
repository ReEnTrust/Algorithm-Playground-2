Overview of E-Recruitment Systems

E-recruitment facilitates employers in terms of selecting the right candidates from a vast pool of candidates. Likewise, it facilitates job seekers in terms of finding revelant jobs efficiently.

Existing e-recruitment systems use keywords or faceted searches to provide better search results to different categories of users (including employers, job seekers, organisations etc.). An e-recruitment process involves postings of job adverts (with job descriptions by employers) and job searches, job applications (by job seekers) as well as job applicants' selection. Various e-recruitment systems such as Indeed (https://www.indeed.co.uk), Monster (https://www.monster.com), and Person force (https://www.personforce.com) use different types algorithms to automate e-recruiment process.

Generally, e-recruitment systems use recommender, prediction and filtering algorithms among others. These algorithms take specific inputs from different categories of users to generate results. For instance, an employer's primary interest is on the ranking of potential candidates for the advertised job position and hence, an employers give specific inputs (such as the required experience, education level etc) to the algorithms to get desribale results. Likewise, a job seeker provides inputs that fits its profile to the algorithms to get desirable results.
