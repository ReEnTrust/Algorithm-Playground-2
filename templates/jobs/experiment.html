E-Recruitment Systems

An e-recruitment system is a web-based tool that automates recruiting processes such as publishing job positions, receiving job applicants' CVs, applicants' pre-screening, ranking and selection among others. An e-recruitment system can be job seekers oriented or recruiters oriented or both. In the first case, an e-recruitment system recommends to the candidate a list of job positions that better fits his profile. In the second case, recruiters publish the specifications of available job positions and the candidates can apply. Existing e-recruitment systems use keywords or faceted searches to provide better search results to different categories of users (including recruiters, job seekers, organisations etc.).

An e-recruitment system as a web-based tool is a merge of algorithms that automates the different requirements of the system. These algorithms take specific inputs from different categories of users to generate results. For instance, a recruiter can provide specific inputs (such as the required experience and education level etc) to the algorithms to rank candidates for an advertised job position. Likewise, a job seeker can provide specific inputs that fits his profile to the algorithms to get desirable job search results.

Generally, e-recruitment systems use recommender, prediction, semantic matching and filtering algorithms among others. For instance, semantic matching algorithms associate semnatically equivalent concepts from the user's CV with the job descriptions, using a dictionary of synonyms. Likewise, filtering algorithms use applicants' personality traits, captured through online forms or predicted for applicants' classification or ranking. E-recruitment systems implement automated candidate ranking based on a set of credible criteria, which are usually specified by job recruiters.

Though an e-recruitment system assist in making recruitment processes more efficient and seemingly less biased through automation, its algorithms rely heavily on data created by humans, and consequently can have human biases carry over to decisions made by the algorithms. This experiment is aimed at having a deeper understanding what algorithmic factors and features may affect users’ perceptions of trust in e-recruitment algorithms.

How Algorithm Playground Works

Algorithm playground provides customised versions of the architecture of e-recruitment algorithms and presents various types of explanations (both technical and non-technical) on the components of e-recruitment algorithms and how the algorithms work. The tool is to develop to examine users’ perception of trust in e-recruitment algorithms and the impact such trust on their attitudes toward the algorithms.

The overall goal of the tool is aimed to help job seekers to look behind the scene, regarding how job applicants may be shortlisted by e-recruitment algorithms. The general public are expected to be more experienced as job seekers than as recruiters. By exposing them about how algorithms may take various factors into consideration to decide how a fictional job applicant is (or not) fit to a given job description, we hope to assess how the study participants perceive the role of algorithms in filtering job applicants, and what would make them trust algorithms more.

Algorithm playground makes you explore different aspects of e-recruitment algorithms. Once a user launches a search for a job on the experiment platform (such as sales manager in London, full time position), then, the implemented job search algorithms returns a list of jobs fitting the search criteria, from the tool local database. Then, the user could choose a job and a fictional job applicant in order to see how a list of fictional job applicants may be rated, according to a given candidate ranking algorithm. Some explanations are provided to help users make sense of the results.

The data collected in this experiment are entirely anonymous. We need your feedback on different aspects of the tool. So, we require your personal opinions. For the task where you need to complete a job application, we provide a fictional profile to be used. At the end of the experiment, click 'feedback' tab to provide your personal feedback on the experiment.
