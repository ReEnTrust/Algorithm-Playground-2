Selection Algorithms

The implemented algorithms ranks job applicants based on some specified parameters. The algorithms assign weights to applicants' attributes and allow recruiters to specify/change parameters that are relevant for applicants' ranking. A recruiter may use one or more of the algorithms. The algorithms allow recruiters to use multiple weighted attributes for applicants' ranking.

Selection Algorithm_1

This algorithm gives the list of all applicants.

Selection Algorithm_2

This algorithm gives highest priority to applicants' location. Here, the algorithm leverages on the location data provided by the applicants and the preference of the recruiter on the expected distance of suitable applicant(s) to rank the applicants.

Selection Algorithm_3

This algorithm gives highest priority to educational level. Here, the qualifications claimed by the applicants are taken as the determinant factors for applicants' ranking.

Selection Algorithm_4

This algorithm gives highest priority to experience. This algorithm checks if the candidate's previous work experience matches up to the requirement specified by the recruiters (if any). Here, consideration is given to skills development, loyalty; average number of years the applicants have spent on their previous jobs.

Selection Algorithm_5

This algorithm allows a recruiter to combine multiple parameters for applicants' ranking. For instance, a recruiter may consider ranking of candidates based on experience and education. Here, applicants' ranking is based on all the parameters specified by the recruiter.
