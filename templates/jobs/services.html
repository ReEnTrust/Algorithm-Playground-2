Selection Algorithms

The implemented algorithms ranks job applicants based on some specified parameters by the recruiters. The algorithms assign scores to applicants' attributes and allow recruiters to specify the attributes to be prioritised. These algorithms allow the use of multiple weighted attributes for applicants' ranking. A recruiter may use one or more of the selection algorithms.

Selection Algorithm_1

This algorithm gives the list of all applicants.

Selection Algorithm_2

This algorithm gives highest priority to applicants' location followed by experience and education respectively. Here, the algorithm leverages on the location data provided by the applicants and the preference of the recruiter on the expected distance of suitable applicant(s) to rank the applicants.

Selection Algorithm_3

This algorithm gives highest priority to educational level followed by experience and location respectively. Here, the qualifications claimed by the applicants are taken as the dominant factors for applicants' ranking.

Selection Algorithm_4

This algorithm gives highest priority to experience followed by education and location respectively. This algorithm checks if the candidate's previous work experience matches up to the requirement specified by the recruiters (if any). Here, consideration is given to skills development, loyalty; average number of years the applicants have spent on their previous jobs.
